Home Chocolate Lava Cake

Chocolate lava cake is a decadent dessert that’s crisp on the outside and gooey on the inside. With a rich, molten chocolate center that oozes out when cut, it’s a chocolate lover’s dream. This recipe is surprisingly simple but delivers a restaurant-quality treat in just minutes. Serve it warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dusting of powdered sugar for an extra touch of indulgence.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 6 oz (170g) dark chocolate, chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • ¼ cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • Cocoa powder or powdered sugar (for dusting)
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Grease four 6-oz ramekins with butter and dust with cocoa powder.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter and chocolate in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolks, and sugar until pale and slightly thickened.
  4. Slowly pour the melted chocolate into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. Add the flour and salt, and mix until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins.
  6. Bake for 12-14 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft.
  7. Let the cakes cool in the ramekins for 1 minute, then carefully invert them onto serving plates.
  8. Dust with powdered sugar or cocoa powder and serve immediately with vanilla ice cream, if desired.
